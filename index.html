Sam Altman

Entrepreneur, Investor, and CEO of OpenAI

Samuel Harris "Sam" Altman, born on April 22, 1985, is a leading American entrepreneur and investor, widely recognized as a pivotal figure in the AI revolution. As the CEO of OpenAI, he has spearheaded the development of groundbreaking technologies like ChatGPT, shaping the future of artificial intelligence.

Early Life & Career

Born in Chicago and raised in St. Louis, Missouri, Sam Altman's fascination with technology began at age eight with his first Apple Macintosh. He attended Stanford University to study computer science but left after two years in 2005 to co-found Loopt, a mobile social networking service. Loopt raised over $30 million in venture capital before being acquired for $43 million.

The Y Combinator Era

Altman joined the renowned startup accelerator Y Combinator in 2011 and became its president in 2014. During his leadership until 2019, he significantly expanded YC's scale and influence, aiming to fund 1,000 companies annually. He introduced new programs like YC Continuity to provide long-term support for scaling startups. Under his guidance, YC nurtured iconic companies such as Airbnb, Stripe, Instacart, DoorDash, Reddit, and Twitch, cementing its status as the world's premier startup accelerator.

Leadership at OpenAI

In 2015, Altman co-founded OpenAI with the mission to ensure artificial general intelligence (AGI) benefits all of humanity. As CEO since 2019, he has overseen the development of transformative AI models. Key achievements include the launch of ChatGPT in 2022, which revolutionized public interaction with AI, and the creation of powerful models like GPT-3, GPT-4, and the image generation model DALL-E.

Ventures & Investments

Sam Altman is a prolific investor with a portfolio valued at over $2.8 billion. He is the chairman of nuclear fusion company Helion Energy and nuclear fission company Oklo Inc. He co-founded Worldcoin, a cryptocurrency project using iris biometrics. His major investments include Reddit, Stripe, Airbnb, Neuralink, and Retro Biosciences, a company focused on extending human life.

Impact & Vision

Altman is a leading voice on the future of AI. He envisions AI as a tool to dramatically accelerate scientific progress and improve quality of life. He describes OpenAI's mission as building a 'brain for the world' and believes AI will lead to an economic transformation. While optimistic, he also advocates for responsible development and has cautioned users about the limitations and potential for 'hallucinations' in current AI models.
